Principal Investigator

Xueqi Li

He is a Ph.D candidate under the supervision of Prof. Ninghui Sun at ICT, CAS. He was with Prof Yuan Xie in SEAL lab at UC Santa Barbara as a reseach scholar from October, 2018 - August, 2020. His research interests include memory-centric architecture, domain-specific architectures, and AI-Bioinformatics. His current research focues on building high-performance, energy-efficient system and architectures for genome analysis.

Doctoral Students

Wanqi Liu

Wanqi Liu is currently a Ph.D. student working under the supervision of Prof. Guangming Tan. After received his B.S. degree in Electronic Information Engineering in 2018, he joined the Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences (ICT, CAS). His research interests include domain-specific architecture (DSA), and hardware accelerator design. His is now focusing on the research of data compression accelerators.

Yewen Li

Yewen Li is currently a Ph.D. student under the supervision of Prof. Guangming Tan. He received the B.S. degree in 2019 from the Department of Information Engineering at Minzu University of China, Beijing, China. His research interests span computer architecture and computational biology. In particular, He is interested in developing algorithms and domain-specific hardware accelerators that enable faster and cheaper progress in biology and medicine.

Prof.

Ninghui Sun

He is a professor at Institute of Computing Technology (ICT), Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). He received his B.S. degree from Peking University in 1989 and M.S. and Ph.D. degrees both in computer science from ICT, CAS in 1992 and 1999, respectively. He is the chief designer of the Dawning series high-performance computers. His research interests include computer architecture, operating systems. He is a fellow of CCF and a member of ACM and IEEE. He is an academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering.

Guangming Tan

He is a professor at Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China. His research interests include high-performance computing, auto-tuning, and bioinformatics. He has published more than 50 paper including SC, PLDI, PPoPP, SPAA, and the IEEE Transactions on Parallel and Distributed Systems.
